Angels and demons — but no, I’m not Dan Brown. This is something quieter, something closer. The city hums at night: neon trembling, shadows stretching, everything alive and slightly unreal.

I walk alone — or at least I think I do. An angel keeps pace beside me, dressed like the world is a runway: Balenciaga on their feet, calm in their eyes. A demon lingers just behind, holding a cold can of Monster Energy White — smiling like they know the ending.

Strange balance, isn’t it? Light that hesitates, darkness that moves without fear!

They don’t speak, but I feel it: a tension, a question hanging in the air. What pulls stronger — control or impulse? What defines us — restraint or desire?

The streetlights flicker: on, off — like a heartbeat. I stop. The angel watches me carefully; the demon tilts their head, almost amused. There’s no rush, no pressure… just choice.

And suddenly it’s clear: they are not enemies — they are reflections.

I take a breath — slow, deliberate.

So who am I in this story?

Not the angel. Not the demon.

Something in between — and maybe that’s the point.
